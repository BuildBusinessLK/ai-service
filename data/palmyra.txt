== PALMYRAH (THAL) PLANT POPULATION BY DISTRICT ==

Palmyrah palm cultivation in Sri Lanka is concentrated mainly in the Northern and Eastern provinces.
Plant population data is available by district from 2019 to 2023.

District-wise Palmyrah plant population:

Jaffna District:
In 2019, Jaffna had 109,000 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Jaffna plant population grew to 121,685.
In 2021, Jaffna plant population dropped significantly to 50,400.
In 2022, Jaffna had the highest recorded population of 271,600 plants.
In 2023, Jaffna plant population was 90,450.

Mannar District:
In 2019, Mannar had 89,221 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Mannar plant population was 81,600.
In 2021, Mannar had 55,000 plants.
In 2022, Mannar had 52,520 plants.
In 2023, Mannar had 14,150 plants, a significant decline.

Vavuniya District:
In 2019, Vavuniya had 91,000 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Vavuniya plant population dropped to 25,030.
In 2021, Vavuniya had 145,000 plants, the highest in the district across the five years.
In 2022, Vavuniya had 10,020 plants.
In 2023, Vavuniya reported nil plants.

Kilinochchi District:
In 2019, Kilinochchi had 12,500 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Kilinochchi had 41,900 plants.
In 2021, Kilinochchi had no recorded palmyrah plants.
In 2022, Kilinochchi had 7,500 plants.
In 2023, Kilinochchi had 11,000 plants.

Mullaitivu District:
In 2019, Mullaitivu had 27,750 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Mullaitivu had 74,700 plants.
In 2021, Mullaitivu had 125,000 plants.
In 2022, Mullaitivu had only 3,750 plants, a sharp decline.
In 2023, Mullaitivu recovered to 32,000 plants.

Trincomalee District:
In 2019, Trincomalee had 2,500 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Trincomalee had 48,870 plants, a major increase.
In 2021, Trincomalee had no recorded palmyrah plants.
In 2022, Trincomalee had 24,000 plants.
In 2023, Trincomalee reported nil plants.

Batticaloa District:
In 2019, Batticaloa had 39,000 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Batticaloa had 58,173 plants.
In 2021, Batticaloa had 100,000 plants.
In 2022, Batticaloa had 36,400 plants.
In 2023, Batticaloa had 60,000 plants, showing recovery.

Amparai District:
In 2019, Amparai had 10,000 palmyrah plants.
In 2020, Amparai had 10,000 plants.
In 2021, Amparai had 25,000 plants.
In 2022, Amparai had 78,600 plants, the highest for this district.
In 2023, Amparai had 20,000 plants.

Hambanthota District:
Hambanthota had no recorded palmyrah plants from 2019 to 2022.
In 2023, Hambanthota recorded 5,000 palmyrah plants for the first time.

Total Palmyrah Plant Population (All Districts):
In 2019, total palmyrah plant population across all districts was 380,971.
In 2020, total plant population grew to 461,958.
In 2021, total plant population reached 500,400, the highest recorded across the five years.
In 2022, total plant population was 484,390.
In 2023, total plant population declined significantly to 232,600, roughly half of the 2021 peak.
Jaffna consistently had the highest or near-highest palmyrah plant population among all districts.
The northern province districts of Jaffna, Mannar, Vavuniya, Kilinochchi, and Mullaitivu dominate palmyrah cultivation.

== KEY INSIGHTS ==

Palmyrah plant populations fluctuate significantly year to year across districts, possibly due to seasonal planting cycles or reporting methods.
The 2023 total plant population of 232,600 was a 53% drop from the 2021 peak of 500,400.
Jaffna district is the largest contributor to palmyrah cultivation in Sri Lanka.
Palmyrah jaggery production dropped by 41% from 2018 to 2019, while treacle production ceased entirely in 2019.
Pulp production more than doubled from 2018 to 2019, suggesting a shift towards pulp-based products.
The palmyrah sector produces a diverse range of products including beverages, sweets, flour-based foods, and savory items.
The sector showed modest revenue growth of 8.5% between 2018 and 2019.
